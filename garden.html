Your Journey Starts Here

Sri Lanka's botanical gardens stand as enchanting havens that beckon foreign visitors with their vibrant beauty and rich history. These meticulously curated sanctuaries offer an immersive journey into the country's diverse flora, providing a window into its ecological significance and natural splendour.

The Peradeniya Royal Botanical Garden, situated near Kandy, is a sprawling paradise that showcases a remarkable array of tropical trees, palms, and orchids. Its "Avenue of Royal Palms" creates a majestic entryway, leading to the breathtaking Great Circle where a massive Java fig tree holds court.

Hakgala Botanical Garden, nestled on the slopes of Hakgala Mountain, offers a captivating blend of temperate and tropical plants. The garden's colourful flower beds and the iconic "Frog and Flower Pond" add to its allure, while its unique climate provides a haven for rare and exotic species.

The Gampaha Henarathgoda Botanical Garden, the country's first of its kind, is a living tribute to Sri Lanka's agricultural history. With its pivotal role in introducing the rubber plant and catalyzing the rubber industry, this garden encapsulates the nation's journey towards economic prosperity.

The botanical gardens of Sri Lanka, whether it's the grandeur of Peradeniya, the unique blend at Hakgala, or the historical significance of Gampaha Henarathgoda, offer foreign visitors an opportunity to discover the island's natural diversity, history, and ecological legacy in a lush and immersive setting.
